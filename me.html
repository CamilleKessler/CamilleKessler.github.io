Hi I'm Camille

Nice to meet you

9 October 2023

Hi, nice to see someone interested in my work! As the title of the website, the website address and the title of this post suggest, I'm Camille Kessler. What these didn't tell you though, is that I am from Switzerland, that I am addicted to (good) chocolate, or that I love dinosaurs and am therefore constantly jealous of boys' clothing options (what kind of adult woman would want to wear a brontosaurus rainjacket or a triceratops Christmas sweater?). Doing a PhD was never actually something I'd thought of before my master's, I seem to have gone with the flow for most of my journey, which unexpectedly actually worked out quite well, I also had the chance to stumble upon the right people at the right time and always had great supervisors.

Being usure of what to do after my bachelor's in Biology, I took a gap year where I worked and volunteered during Cape Verde's turtle nesting season. I wanted to do a master's but found no interesting options in Switzerland and going abroad would be too expensive. When time had come to apply, though, a new Prof. had arrived at the Université de Neuchâtel, where I had done my bachelor's. It was Daniel Croll, who created the Laboratory of Evolutionary Genetics. Now that sounded very interesting! I joined the hybrid Biology program (one year of courses and one year of thesis), and worked with Daniel Croll and Christine Grossen on alpine ibex population genetics. This is when I realised that genomics and bioinformatics were awesome and that that's what I wanted to do with my life. As a result of this newfound interest, my grades increased dramatically and I graduated from my master's with a magna cum laude distinction. After my master's, again, I was usure of what to do. I had found no PhD project of interest so I joined the Swiss Institute of Bioinformatics in Lausanne to work as data analyst trainee. While this intership was a very good opportunity to develop my bioinformatics skills, it was also helpful in telling me what I did not want to do with my life: biomedical reseach on human RNA data.

Currently, I am a PhD candidate in the Environmental and Life Sciences graduate program at Trent University in Ontario, Canada, where I am studying the speciation genomics of two North American deer species: white-tailed deer (Odocoileus virginianus) and mule deer (O. hemionus). You can read more about my PhD project here, about my master's project here and about everything else here.
